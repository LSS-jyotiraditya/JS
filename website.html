The items shown on this slide are the intricate handicrafts made by our local artisans. You have probably purchased it at some point in your life, but do you think the craftsman received all the money? Unfortunately not, most of the money was profited by middlemen.

According to The Economic Survey, 93% of the India's workforce include the self-employed. In contrast to the National minimum wage of ₹4,576/month, local Craftsmen only earn an average of ₹2,000/month and over 30% are in debt. The problems faced by them are less exposure to information and technology, proper funding, and marketing support.

We cater solely to local craftsmen and helps advertise their goods. There is a direct line of trade between craftsmen and customers which enables craftsmen to receive 100% of their profits. We enable craftsmen to sell to the urban market in a streamlined manner. We also allow customers to donate to craftsmen. Donations can be anonymous at the donor's discretion.

About

Despite having over 200 million craftsmen, India only contributes 2% to the global handicrafts market worth over $663.9 Billion. We hope to improve these figures with an effective action plan.

Our strengths include working at the grassroots level to build a loyal base and strong sense of community. However, there is a lack of access to technology for a major segment of our target base. We have several opportunities because the market we are aiming for is largely untapped. However, we face the threat of mass-manufactured low-cost products.

The idea is to basically increase india's contribution to the world's handicrafts industry while at the same time protect local craftsmen from getting ripped off. NextCraft is not a typical amazon or flipkart like e-commerce site , it is just a platform connecting the buyers to the sellers .
